Drag from the blue (positive) or red (negative) Thumbs Up sign to a node (circle). An arrow should appear. Drag the end of the arrow to another node. You have voted for a positive or negative influence from the first node to the second. If no connection existed previously, one should appear. If connection existed the line may change colour, depending on whether your vote caused the connection strength to cross a threshold.

The colour of the nodes reflects the tendency of the 'stock' of the nodes to go up (blue) or down (red), depending on the votes received.

In order for your votes to count, and to be saved between sessions, you must login. However you can still play with the basic functionality without logging in.

Metrocene is derived from work done on climate adaptation for CMRC. The Iasess prototype was originally developed with the sponsorship of the EPA as part of the CLAD and ICIP projects.

The prototype uses Fuzzy Cognitive Mapping to develop a model of a system and its outcomes.

It is written in D3 using ClojureScript via the Strokes interop library. Clojure core.async is used to bring a little sanity to the event handling.
